Frequently Asked Questions

What are dental implants?

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots made from titanium that are surgically placed into the jawbone to support replacement teeth or bridges.

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last for many years, often a lifetime, making them a durable solution for missing teeth.

Are dental implants safe?

Yes, dental implants are considered a safe and effective treatment for replacing missing teeth, with a high success rate when performed by experienced professionals.

What is the implant process like?

The process involves several stages: consultation, preparation, implant placement, healing, and restoration with a custom crown, all designed to ensure optimal outcomes.

Is the implant procedure painful?

Most patients report minimal discomfort during the procedure, which is typically managed with local anesthesia and sedation options if needed.

Can anyone get dental implants?

Most adults with good oral and general health are candidates for implants. A consultation can determine if implants are right for you.

How do I care for my dental implants?

Caring for implants involves regular brushing and flossing, along with routine dental check-ups to ensure they remain in good condition.

What are the benefits of dental implants?

Implants offer a natural appearance, improved functionality, durability, and help maintain bone health by preventing bone loss.

How much do dental implants cost?

The cost varies depending on the number of implants needed and other factors. We offer competitive pricing and flexible payment options.

Will my insurance cover dental implants?

Coverage varies by insurance provider. We recommend checking with your insurer and we can assist with documentation if needed.

How long does the implant process take?

The entire process can take several months, allowing time for the implant to integrate with the jawbone and for custom restoration.

What if I don't have enough bone for an implant?

In cases of insufficient bone, procedures like bone grafting can be performed to build up the jawbone for successful implant placement.

What are the risks associated with dental implants?

While generally safe, risks include infection, nerve damage, and implant failure, which are minimized with proper care and experienced professionals.

Can implants be used for multiple missing teeth?

Yes, implants can support multiple teeth or even full-mouth restorations, providing a versatile solution for various dental needs.

What materials are used for dental implants?

Implants are typically made from titanium, a biocompatible material that integrates well with bone, ensuring stability and longevity.

How do implants compare to dentures?

Unlike dentures, implants are permanent, offer better functionality, and help maintain jawbone health, making them a preferred choice for many.

What should I expect after the implant procedure?

Post-procedure, you may experience some swelling and discomfort, which typically resolves within a few days. Follow-up care ensures proper healing.

Can implants fail?

While rare, implant failure can occur due to factors like infection or insufficient bone integration. Regular check-ups help mitigate such risks.
