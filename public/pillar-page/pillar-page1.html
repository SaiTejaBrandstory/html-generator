Types of Motor Insurance

Motor insurance can be broadly classified into the following coverage types:

Car Insurance

A car insurance policy offers coverage against damages to your own car from accidents, fire, natural calamities and other unforeseen events. It also covers third party (TP) liabilities caused in an accident involving the insured car. While choosing car insurance, you should always compare policy premiums offered by various insurers to get the best deal.

The premium amount would majorly depend on the car's make & value, the state of registration, and its manufacturing year.

Bike Insurance

Bike insurance provides financial protection for your two-wheeler against damages, theft, and third-party liabilities. It's mandatory in India to have at least third-party bike insurance to ride legally on public roads.

Electric Car Insurance

Electric car insurance covers your electric vehicle against damages, theft, and third-party liabilities. It also includes coverage for the battery pack and charging equipment, which are specific to electric vehicles.

Commercial Vehicle Insurance

Commercial vehicle insurance provides coverage for vehicles used for business purposes, including trucks, vans, and delivery vehicles. It covers damages, theft, and third-party liabilities specific to commercial use.

Types of Vehicle Insurance Policy in India

The main three types of motor vehicle insurance policies offered in India include:

Third-Party Car Insurance

Third party liability only insurance coverage is a mandatory vehicle insurance policy under the Indian Motor Vehicles Act, 1988.

It covers third person injuries or death in case of an accident involving the insured vehicle. However, third party insurance doesn't provide any direct benefit to the policyholder's vehicle.

Standalone OD Cover

Standalone Own Damage (OD) cover provides protection for damages to your own vehicle from accidents, natural calamities, theft, and fire. It does not include third-party liability coverage.

Comprehensive Insurance Cover

Comprehensive insurance cover provides complete protection for your vehicle, including both own damage and third-party liability coverage. It offers the most extensive coverage available for motor vehicles in India.

Coverages Under Motor Insurance

What is Covered in Policy

Third-party Liabilities

Covers third-party liabilities, including third-party death, disability and property damages.

Accidents or Collisions

Covers damages to your vehicle resulting from accidents or collisions with other vehicles or objects.

Vehicle Theft

Provides financial coverage in case your vehicle is stolen or hijacked.

Natural or Man-Made Acts

Covers damages caused by natural disasters like floods, earthquakes, cyclones, or man-made disasters like riots, strikes, and terrorism.

Fire

Covers damages to your vehicle caused by fire, explosion, or self-ignition.

Animal Attacks

Covers damages caused by collisions with animals or damage from animal attacks.

In-transit Damages

Covers damages to your vehicle while it is being transported by road, rail, inland waterway, lift, or elevator.

What is Not Covered in Policy

Vehicle Depreciation

Motor insurance doesn't cover the usual wear and tear of the insured four-wheeler and its parts.

Drunk Driving

Any damages or accidents caused while driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs are not covered.

Outside Geographical Area

Damages occurring outside the geographical area specified in the policy are not covered.

Invalid Driving License

Accidents or damages occurring when the driver doesn't have a valid driving license are not covered.

Mechanical Failure

Mechanical or electrical breakdowns, failures, or damages to the vehicle's parts due to normal wear and tear are not covered.

Illegal Driving

Damages occurring while the vehicle is being used for illegal purposes or in violation of traffic rules are not covered.

Damage due to War

Damages caused by war, invasion, act of foreign enemy, hostilities, civil war, rebellion, revolution, or insurrection are not covered.

Factors That Affect Vehicle Insurance Premiums

When determining the motor insurance premium, insurance companies consider the following factors:

Makes & Model

The premium of your motor insurance plan depends upon your vehicle's make and model, cubic capacity, and variant.

Age

The depreciation value, as well as the insured declared value, depends upon the age of your motor vehicle.

Fuel

The fuel type whether it is a petrol/diesel engine or an electric motor will affect the IDV that might increase the premium amount.

Location

The geographical location where your vehicle is registered and primarily used affects the premium calculation.

Driving History

Your past driving record, including any claims or traffic violations, can significantly impact your insurance premium.

No Claim Bonus

A no-claim bonus (NCB) can reduce your premium if you haven't made any claims in the previous policy year.

Add-ons

Optional add-on covers like zero depreciation, engine protection, or roadside assistance will increase your premium.

How to File a Motor Insurance Claim?

Raising a motor insurance claim involves some formalities and documentation, which depend upon the type of vehicle and nature of the loss.

Here are different ways to file a motor insurance claim for your vehicle:

Inform the insurance company immediately after the accident.

Provide a detailed estimate of the damage to the insurer along with claim documents.

A surveyor is then appointed to inspect the damaged vehicle.

The insurer will review the survey report.

Upon approval from the insurer, take the insured vehicle to a network garage for repairs.

The insurance company will settle the claim directly with the garage.

File an FIR with the police immediately after the incident and keep a copy with you.

Gather the third-party motor insurance details of the vehicle owner.

Obtain a copy of the police charge sheet filed against the vehicle owner.

Hire a motor claims lawyer and file the compensation case in MACT (Motor Accident Claims Tribunal).

If the vehicle owner suggests an out-of-court settlement, decide the settlement amount mutually.

In case of damage to your property, file the claim in the local tribunal court.

The court decides or rejects the compensation after verifying the documents and hearing the story from both parties (you and the other vehicle owner).

The maximum compensation amount in case of property damage is Rs. 7.5 Lakhs.

FAQs About Motor Insurance

Q1. Which vehicle insurance plan should I purchase- comprehensive insurance plan or third-party insurance plan only?

+

The choice between comprehensive and third-party insurance depends on your needs. Comprehensive insurance provides coverage for both your vehicle and third-party liabilities, while third-party insurance only covers damages to third parties. For better protection, comprehensive insurance is recommended.

Q2. How is the motor insurance premium calculated?

+

Motor insurance premium is calculated based on several factors including the vehicle's make, model, age, IDV (Insured Declared Value), location, type of coverage, add-ons selected, and your claim history. Premiums vary between insurers and policies.

Q3. What is the period of the motor insurance policy?

+

Motor insurance policies are typically issued for a period of one year. However, you can also opt for long-term policies (2-3 years) for new vehicles, which may offer discounts and convenience.

Q4. What is a deductible in a motor insurance policy?

+

A deductible is the amount you agree to pay out of pocket before your insurance coverage kicks in. It helps reduce your premium. There are two types: compulsory deductible (mandatory) and voluntary deductible (optional, chosen by you).

Q5. What is the process for getting the changes done in the policy?

+

To make changes to your policy, contact your insurer or visit their website/app. Common changes include address updates, adding/removing add-ons, or updating vehicle details. You may need to submit relevant documents and pay any additional premium if applicable.

Q6. If I lose my motor insurance policy, would I get a duplicate copy?

+

Yes, you can get a duplicate copy of your motor insurance policy by contacting your insurer. You'll need to provide your policy number, vehicle details, and may need to pay a nominal fee. Most insurers also provide digital copies through their online portals.

Q7. Can I get my motor insurance transferred to the buyer of my automobile?

+

Yes, motor insurance can be transferred to the new owner when you sell your vehicle. The buyer needs to inform the insurer about the transfer and provide necessary documents. The remaining policy period and No Claim Bonus (NCB) can be transferred to the new owner.

Q8. What happens to my motor insurance if I sell my vehicle?

+

When you sell your vehicle, you can either transfer the policy to the new owner or cancel it and get a refund for the unused portion. If you're buying a new vehicle, you may be able to transfer your No Claim Bonus to the new policy.

Q1. What happens if I don't buy motor insurance for my vehicle?

+

Driving without at least third-party motor insurance is illegal in India. You may face penalties, fines, and legal consequences. Additionally, you'll be personally liable for any damages or injuries caused in an accident, which can result in significant financial burden.

Q2. Is buying a motor insurance policy easy?

+

Yes, buying motor insurance is now very easy, especially online. You can compare policies, get quotes, and purchase insurance within minutes through insurance aggregator websites or directly from insurer websites. The process is simple and requires basic vehicle and personal information.

Q3. Is it beneficial to buy a motor insurance policy online?

+

Yes, buying motor insurance online offers several benefits including easy comparison of multiple policies, competitive premiums, instant policy issuance, 24/7 availability, paperless process, and often better discounts. You can also manage and renew your policy conveniently online.

Q4. Can I renew my motor insurance online?

+

Yes, you can easily renew your motor insurance online through the insurer's website or app, or through insurance aggregator platforms. The process is quick, and you can compare different renewal options, add or remove add-ons, and complete the renewal in just a few minutes.

Q5. When is pre-policy vehicle inspection carried out?

+

Pre-policy vehicle inspection is typically carried out for older vehicles (usually 5+ years), vehicles with high IDV, or when there are discrepancies in the information provided. The inspection helps the insurer assess the vehicle's condition and determine the appropriate premium.

Q1. If I am driving my car in a specific city, how would the premium rate would be applied?

+

Motor insurance premiums are calculated based on the vehicle's registered location (RTO). The premium rate depends on the city's zone classification (Zone A, B, or C) which is determined by factors like vehicle density, accident rates, and theft statistics. Premiums are higher in metro cities compared to smaller towns.

Q2. Is there any kind of discounts that would help reduce my policy premium?

+

Yes, several discounts can help reduce your premium including No Claim Bonus (NCB) for claim-free years, voluntary deductible, anti-theft device installation, membership in automobile associations, long-term policy purchase, and online purchase discounts. Always check with your insurer for available discounts.

Q3. Is service tax applicable on the motor insurance premium?

+

Service tax has been replaced by GST (Goods and Services Tax). Currently, GST at 18% is applicable on motor insurance premiums. This tax is included in the total premium amount you pay. The GST component is clearly mentioned in your policy document.

Q4. In case I fit LPG or CNG kit in my automobile, is it necessary to update the insurance provider about that?

+

Yes, it is mandatory to inform your insurance provider if you install an LPG or CNG kit in your vehicle. This modification affects the vehicle's risk profile and premium. You may need to pay additional premium, and failure to inform may result in claim rejection in case of an accident.

Q5. What do you mean by electronic/electrical accessories in cars?

+

Electronic/electrical accessories refer to additional equipment installed in your vehicle that are not part of the original manufacturer's specifications, such as music systems, GPS devices, dash cameras, additional lighting, etc. These need to be declared separately and may require additional coverage or premium.

Q6. Who can get personal accident cover in case of cars?

+

Personal accident cover is available for the owner-driver of the vehicle. It provides coverage in case of death or permanent disability due to an accident while driving the insured vehicle. You can opt for this cover as part of your comprehensive motor insurance policy, and it's mandatory for commercial vehicles.

Q1. What should I do in case of an accident?

+

In case of an accident, first ensure everyone's safety and call for medical help if needed. Inform the police and file an FIR if required. Take photos of the accident scene and damage. Contact your insurance company immediately to register the claim. Do not admit fault or make any statements without consulting your insurer.

Q2. Is it possible for me to cancel my motor insurance claim?

+

Yes, you can withdraw or cancel your motor insurance claim before it is settled. However, you need to inform your insurer in writing. Note that withdrawing a claim may affect your No Claim Bonus, and you'll be responsible for all repair costs. Check with your insurer about the specific process and implications.

Q3. What is No Claim Bonus in motor Insurance?

+

No Claim Bonus (NCB) is a discount on your premium for each claim-free year. It starts at 20% after the first claim-free year and can go up to 50% after 5 consecutive claim-free years. NCB is transferable when you switch insurers or buy a new vehicle, making it a valuable benefit for safe drivers.

Q4. In case I change my motor insurance provider, would my No Claim Bonus be migrated?

+

Yes, your No Claim Bonus (NCB) can be transferred to your new insurance provider. You need to provide the NCB certificate or renewal notice from your previous insurer as proof. The new insurer will apply the same NCB percentage to your new policy, helping you maintain the discount you've earned.
